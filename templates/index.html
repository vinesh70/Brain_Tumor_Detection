Brain Tumor Detection

Welcome to our Brain Tumor Detection System. Brain tumors are abnormal growths of tissue within the brain. They can be benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous) and can affect various parts of the brain.

Common types of brain tumors include:

  • 1) Glioma Tumor: These tumors arise from glial cells and can be found in the brain or spinal cord. They include astrocytomas, oligodendrogliomas, and ependymomas.
  • 2) Meningioma Tumor: Meningiomas form in the meninges, the thin layers of tissue that cover the brain and spinal cord. They are usually benign and slow-growing.
  • 3) Pituitary Tumor: Pituitary tumors develop in the pituitary gland, a small gland located at the base of the brain. They can cause hormonal imbalances and affect various bodily functions.
